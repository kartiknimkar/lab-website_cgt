Decoding membrane trafficking and extracellular vesicles in cancer biology

Led by Dr. Emanuele Cocucci, MD, PhD, the lab studies how cells internalize signals, exchange information through extracellular vesicles, and how these pathways can be targeted for better cancer therapies and drug delivery.

Research Themes Featured Papers

20+ years in membrane biology

Brief Summary

Endocytosis & Membrane Dynamics

Investigating the mechanisms of clathrin-mediated endocytosis and membrane scission, including dynamin recruitment and vesicle formation in live cells.

Extracellular Vesicles

Understanding ectosomes and other extracellular vesicles as communication channels in tumor microenvironments and disease progression.

Cancer & Nanovector Delivery

Defining how membrane trafficking differs in cancer and improving delivery/internalization strategies for therapeutic nanovectors.

Research Sections

Single-Molecule Quantitative Imaging

High-resolution microscopy and quantitative analysis to map vesicle assembly, molecular stoichiometry, and trafficking kinetics.

Mechanisms of Vesicle Biogenesis

Tracking how endocytic and extracellular vesicles form and scission events are coordinated across space and time.

Translational Direction

Translating trafficking biology into actionable targets for anti-cancer treatment and precision drug delivery.
